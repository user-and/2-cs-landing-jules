HOW OFTEN SHOULD I CHECK MY CREDIT?

It's wise to check your credit reports frequently for signs of fraud or identity theft. Common types of identity theft include fraudulent credit cards and loans, all of which will show up in your credit profile. Early detection is the key to avoiding long-term financial consequences.

COMMON TYPES OF IDENTITY THEFT

CREDIT CARDS

Credit card fraud occurs when there is unauthorized use of one's credit card account.

UTILITY FRAUD

Utility fraud occurs when stolen personal and banking information is used to open new utility accounts.

LOANS

Loan fraud involves using another person's personal and credit information to obtain new loans.

WHAT IS FACTA?

The Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions Act (or "FACTA") is a law passed in 2003 that entitles each American to a free credit report each year from each credit bureau (TransUnion, Equifax, and Experian). Limitations include the fact that free credit scores are not included in the program and that you can only receive one free credit report from each credit bureau every 12 months.
